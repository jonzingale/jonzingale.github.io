Schelling Segregation

The study of complex systems is designed to advance our scientific frontiers by exploring how the interactions of the parts of system can lead to unexpected behavior. Consider a social network---while an individual may be driven by a variety of motives in deciding what personal information to share or news to pass on to others---it is the aggregations of these individual actions that produce unexpected viral videos and social revolutions. These, and many other systems, embody complexity that cannot be understood by only knowing the behavior of any given part.

Complex systems explains how in a world of individuals that are tolerant of one another, segregation can still emerge.
